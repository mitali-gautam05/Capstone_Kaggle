## Summary

**Gate Exam Notes**

**What is the Gate Exam?**

The Gate Exam is a competitive exam for admission to postgraduate programs in engineering and technology in India. It is conducted by the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) and Indian Institute of Science (IISc) on a rotational basis.

**Key Points:**

1. **Eligibility Criteria**: The candidate must have a bachelor's degree in engineering or technology from a recognized university with a valid GATE score.
2. **Exam Pattern**: The GATE exam consists of multiple-choice questions and numerical answer type questions in various subjects like engineering, mathematics, and science.
3. **Exam Duration**: The exam is conducted in an online mode and has a duration of 3 hours.
4. **Exam Subjects**: The exam is conducted in various subjects like Aerospace Engineering, Biotechnology, Chemical Engineering, Civil Engineering, Computer Science and Information Technology, Electrical Engineering, and more.
5. **Score Validity**: The GATE score is valid for 3 years from the date of declaration of the result.

**Example:**

Suppose a student, Rohan, wants to pursue a master's degree in computer science from IIT Bombay. He appears for the GATE exam in computer science and technology and scores well. He can use his GATE score to apply for admission to IIT Bombay's master's program in computer science.

**Summary:**

The Gate Exam is a competitive exam for admission to postgraduate programs in engineering and technology in India. It is conducted by the IITs and IISc on a rotational basis. The exam has a duration of 3 hours and is conducted in various subjects. A valid GATE score is required for admission to IITs and IISc.
