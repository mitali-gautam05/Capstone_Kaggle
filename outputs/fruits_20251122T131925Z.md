## Summary

**Fruits Study Notes**

**Definition/Overview:**
Fruits are the sweet and fleshy parts of plants that grow from flowers and contain seeds. They are a vital source of essential nutrients, vitamins, and minerals that are necessary for a healthy diet.

**Key Points:**

1. **Types of Fruits:** There are many different types of fruits, including:
	* Citrus fruits (oranges, lemons, limes)
	* Berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
	* Tropical fruits (mangoes, pineapples, bananas)
	* Stone fruits (peaches, plums, cherries)
2. **Functions of Fruits:** Fruits serve several important functions, including:
	* Reproduction: Fruits contain seeds that can grow into new plants.
	* Food source: Fruits are a nutritious food source for humans and animals.
	* Dispersal: Fruits help to disperse seeds to new locations.
3. **Nutritional Value:** Fruits are rich in essential nutrients, including:
	* Vitamins (vitamin C, vitamin A)
	* Minerals (potassium, magnesium)
	* Fiber
4. **Examples of Fruits:** Some common examples of fruits include:
	* Apples
	* Bananas
	* Oranges
	* Grapes
5. **Seasonal Fruits:** Fruits are often seasonal, meaning they are available at different times of the year. For example:
	* Strawberries are available in the spring
	* Tomatoes are available in the summer
	* Apples are available in the fall

**Example:**
A simple example of a fruit is an apple. Apples are a type of fruit that grows on apple trees and are a good source of fiber and antioxidants.

**Summary:**
Fruits are an essential part of a healthy diet, providing essential nutrients, vitamins, and minerals. They come in many different types and serve several important functions, including reproduction, food source, and dispersal. By understanding the different types of fruits and their nutritional value, we can make informed choices about the fruits we eat.
