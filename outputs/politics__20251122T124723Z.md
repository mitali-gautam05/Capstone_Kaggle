**Definition**

An ecosystem is a community of living organisms (plants, animals, and microbes) that interact with each other and their non-living environment (air, water, soil, and sunlight). This interaction creates a complex system that supports life and maintains the balance of nature.

**Explanation**

Ecosystems can be found in various environments, such as forests, grasslands, deserts, and oceans. Each ecosystem has its unique characteristics, including temperature, humidity, and nutrient availability, which support the growth and survival of its inhabitants. The relationships within an ecosystem are influenced by factors like predation, competition, symbiosis, and decomposition.

**Key Points**

- **Components**: Living organisms (biotic factors) and non-living factors (abiotic factors)
- **Interactions**: Predation, competition, symbiosis, decomposition
- **Ecological Balance**: The delicate balance between living and non-living components that supports life
- **Adaptation**: Organisms adapt to their environment through evolution and natural selection

**Examples**

- **Forest Ecosystem**: Trees provide shade and shelter for animals, while animals help disperse seeds and maintain soil quality.
- **Grassland Ecosystem**: Grasses and wildflowers compete for sunlight and water, while herbivores and carnivores interact in a predator-prey relationship.

**Final Condensed Summary**

An ecosystem is a complex system of living and non-living components that interact to support life. Key points include:

- Living organisms (biotic factors) and non-living factors (abiotic factors)
- Interactions like predation, competition, symbiosis, and decomposition
- Ecological balance and adaptation through evolution and natural selection.

Remember, ecosystems are essential for maintaining the balance of nature and supporting life on Earth.
